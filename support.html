Getting Started
Commit is a macOS menu bar app that tracks your daily deep work hours against a commitment you set. Unlike Pomodoro timers, Commit doesn't care about individual sessions — it tracks your cumulative total for the day and shows whether you're ahead or behind your goal.
Click the Commit icon in your menu bar and open Settings. You'll see a slider to set your daily commitment anywhere from 30 minutes to 8 hours. Your goal applies to all active days going forward.
The menu bar shows your current status in real time:

• −1h 26m — you still have 1 hour and 26 minutes to go to reach your goal
• +0h 45m — you've exceeded your goal by 45 minutes
• Done — you've hit your goal exactly

During an active session, the timer updates every minute.
Commit requires macOS 13 Ventura or later. It works on both Intel and Apple Silicon Macs.

Pro Features
With deficit rollover (a Pro feature), if you fall short of your goal today, the shortfall is carried over and added to tomorrow's goal. For example, if your goal is 4 hours and you only logged 3, tomorrow's goal becomes 5 hours. The rollover is capped at 2× your daily goal to prevent snowballing.
Tap "Go Pro" inside the app. You'll see the subscription options — a 3-day free trial is offered to new subscribers who have never subscribed before. Apple handles the trial and billing. You won't be charged until the trial ends.
All subscriptions are managed by Apple. To cancel:

1. Open System Settings (or System Preferences) on your Mac
2. Click your Apple ID
3. Go to Subscriptions
4. Find Commit and click Cancel

You can also manage subscriptions at apps.apple.com/account/subscriptions.
Try restoring your purchases: open the Commit menu bar popover, tap the settings icon, and choose "Restore Purchases." If that doesn't work, email us at simpletechman642@gmail.com with your App Store receipt and we'll sort it out.
Privacy & Data
No. All your data is stored locally on your Mac using Apple's SwiftData framework. Commit has no servers, no user accounts, and sends no data anywhere. iCloud sync is not available in the current version.
Commit collects no personal data. The only data that exists is your session history stored locally in the app's sandbox on your device. See our Privacy Policy for full details.
Uninstalling the app from your Mac removes all locally stored data. You can also delete individual sessions from within the app's history view.
Troubleshooting
Your menu bar may be full. Try holding Command (⌘) and dragging other icons to make space, or check if the icon is hidden by clicking the Control Center icon. If the problem persists, try quitting and relaunching Commit from your Applications folder.
Commit calculates your total based on session durations stored locally. If you changed your Mac's system clock or traveled across time zones during an active session, the time may appear off. Stopping and starting a new session should reset the display. If the issue persists, email us.
